Volcanic Island

This scene was a test for large scale environments. I started the year by making mountains procedurally with displacement nodes, but that proved to have its drawbacks. For this scene I went to the Noob to Pro/Landscape Modeling tutorial by Wikibooks for manual mountain making.

The main principle demonstrated in this tutorial was proportional editing. Proportional editing is a tool that allows you to manipulate multiple verticies at once based on a mathematical falloff rather than manipulating one vertex at a time. By setting the falloff to a gradual curve, you can manually create smooth peaks from a subdivided plane.

By setting the falloff of the proportional editing tool to random, you can add fine detail to the rocky surface.

Using these tools, a whole bunch of mountains can be created very quickly. I decided that mountains were boring so I turned mine into a volcano. I used proportional editing to carve a hole into the top and then I added a red light source to emulate lava.
